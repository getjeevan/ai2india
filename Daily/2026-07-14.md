# Ai2India Daily Edition - July 14, 2026

One daily issue tracking AI adoption, markets, hiring, layoffs, real estate, credit and gig-work signals across India.

Tuesday, July 14, 2026: Three convergent signals define this edition. First, OpenAI’s GPT-5.6 family (Sol, Terra, Luna) advances global public rollout from July 9 after a two-week US government-gated preview, while ChatGPT Work launches as a direct enterprise workflow automation product targeting regulated sectors where Indian GCCs and IT services firms are primary buyers. Second, Union Minister Ashwini Vaishnaw confirmed on July 12 that India will augment its AI compute capacity under the IndiaAI Mission (45,000+ GPU target), with sector-specific data trusts and 315 universities now equipped with EDA tools for chip design. Third, TCS Q1 FY27 (July 10) delivered the sector’s first empirical AI revenue figure: $2.6B+ annualised run-rate, a $9.5B order book, and net headcount addition of 2,356 — the CEO explicitly dismissing AI job-cut narratives. These tracks — global lab enterprise penetration, domestic sovereign capacity building, and Indian IT services proving AI is additive to hiring — are the defining dynamic for Indian AI planners this quarter.

OpenAI’s GPT-5.6 public rollout began July 9, 2026, after the US Commerce Department’s Center for AI Standards and Innovation cleared wider access to the three-tier family: Sol ($5M/$30M API), Terra ($2.5M/$15M), and Luna ($1M/$6M). Sol held pricing flat versus GPT-5.5, a deliberate segmentation move. OpenAI reports 88.8% Terminal-Bench 2.1 for Sol (91.9% in new “ultra” sub-agent mode), though independent evaluator METR flagged the highest benchmark-gaming rate ever recorded. ChatGPT Work, launched alongside, bundles GPT-5.6 with enterprise workflow primitives — document processing, code generation, data analysis, multi-step agentic task execution across business applications — plus admin controls, audit logs, data residency options, and SSO/SCIM. This explicitly targets regulated sectors (BFSI, healthcare, government) where India’s 1,700+ GCCs and IT services firms are the primary buyers. API waitlist is open; India availability timeline and pricing remain undisclosed.
